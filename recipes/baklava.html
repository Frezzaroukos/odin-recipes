Baklava

Description

Baklava is a rich, sweet pastry made of layers of phyllo dough filled with chopped nuts and sweetened with syrup or honey. This dessert has a long history in the Mediterranean and Middle East regions and is particularly popular in Greek cuisine.

The combination of flaky pastry, crunchy nuts, and sweet syrup creates an irresistible treat that's perfect for special occasions or as a sweet ending to any meal.

Ingredients

  • 500g phyllo dough sheets
  • 400g mixed nuts (walnuts, almonds, pistachios), finely chopped
  • 250g butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • For the syrup:
  • 350g sugar
  • 300ml water
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 strip of lemon peel
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C (325°F).
  2. In a bowl, mix the chopped nuts with cinnamon, cloves, and 2 tablespoons of sugar.
  3. Brush a 23x33cm (9x13 inch) baking dish with melted butter.
  4. Layer 6 sheets of phyllo in the dish, brushing each sheet with butter before adding the next.
  5. Sprinkle 1/3 of the nut mixture evenly over the phyllo.
  6. Add 4 more sheets of phyllo, brushing each with butter, then another 1/3 of the nuts.
  7. Repeat with 4 more buttered phyllo sheets and the remaining nuts.
  8. Top with 6-8 sheets of phyllo, brushing each with butter, including the top sheet.
  9. With a sharp knife, carefully cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes, cutting all the way to the bottom.
  10. Bake for 50-60 minutes, until golden and crisp.
  11. While the baklava is baking, make the syrup: combine sugar, water, cinnamon stick, cloves, and lemon peel in a saucepan.
  12. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  13. Remove from heat, discard the spices and lemon peel, and stir in honey and lemon juice.
  14. When the baklava is done, remove from the oven and immediately pour the hot syrup evenly over it.
  15. Let cool completely, uncovered, for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the syrup to be absorbed.
